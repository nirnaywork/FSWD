Chicken Biriyani

Description Of Chicken Biriyani

Chicken Biryani is a fragrant and flavorful South Asian rice dish made with aromatic basmati rice, tender chicken, and a blend of rich spices. The chicken is first marinated in yogurt and spices, then cooked with caramelized onions, tomatoes, and herbs to create a deep, savory masala. Layers of partially cooked basmati rice are added on top, along with saffron milk, fried onions, and fresh herbs. The dish is then slow-cooked (dum style) to let the flavors blend beautifully. Each spoonful offers a perfect balance of juicy chicken, spiced rice, and aromatic goodness — a true celebration of flavor and tradition.

Ingredients

  • 500 g basmati rice
  • 600 g chicken, cut into medium pieces
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup yogurt (curd)
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 3-4 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • Whole spices: 2 bay leaves, 4 cloves, 2 cardamoms, 1-inch cinnamon stick, 1 star anise
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1½ tsp garam masala powder or biryani masala
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 4 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as required
  • Saffron soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk (optional)

Steps to prepare Chicken Biriyani!

  1. Wash and soak basmati rice in water for 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. In a bowl, marinate chicken with yogurt, half of the ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, turmeric, salt, lemon juice, and a little garam masala. Let it rest for 30 minutes or more.
  3. In a large pot, heat oil or ghee and fry onions until golden brown. Remove half for garnishing.
  4. Add whole spices to the remaining onions, then add rest of the ginger-garlic paste and sauté until aromatic.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes, mint, and coriander leaves. Cook until tomatoes soften.
  6. Add marinated chicken and cook until it’s tender and the masala thickens.
  7. In another pot, boil water with salt and a few drops of oil. Add soaked rice and cook until 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  8. Layer the biryani: Spread half of the cooked rice over the chicken, then add fried onions, mint, coriander, and saffron milk. Repeat for remaining rice.
  9. Cover tightly and cook on low heat (dum) for 15–20 minutes.
  10. Fluff gently and serve hot with raita or salad.
